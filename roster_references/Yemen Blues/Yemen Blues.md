**Yemen Blues** defies category—an electrifying collision of cultures, eras, and languages that forms one of the most original sounds in global music today. Founded in 2010 by visionary vocalist and composer **Ravid Kahalani**, the group draws on Yemenite, Jewish, and Arabic traditions while freely bending them into new shapes. Their music is a seamless hybrid of funk, highlife, blues, Bedouin folk, gnawa trance, hip-hop, psych, jazz, and ecstatic soul, all propelled by Kahalani’s raw, shape-shifting vocals in Hebrew, Arabic, French, Haitian Creole, and invented dialects.

Built around the alchemy of its core members—**Kahalani**, percussionist **Rony Iwryn**, bassist/oud master **Shanir Blumenkranz**, and drummer **Dan Mayo**—Yemen Blues operates like a four-way spiritual engine. Hypnotic polyrhythms, deep desert melodies, and explosive improvisation collide to create a sound that feels ancient yet boldly contemporary. Their latest work, including *Only Love Remains* and *Shabazai – A Tribute to the Poet*, pushes them into grittier, more electrifying territory, delivering a global soundtrack for modern catharsis.

Onstage, Yemen Blues is a storm—horns blazing, drums thundering, voices soaring. At its core, the band is driven by a belief in music’s unifying power: spirituality, freedom, and love woven into every note. It sounds timeless. It sounds like today.
